How to Use ModuleSIM

Solar Panel Power Output Simulator · ENGY7000

Overview

ModuleSIM estimates the annual electrical energy output (kWh) of a flat-plate solar PV module under clear-sky conditions. It calculates direct and diffuse solar irradiance at 12-minute intervals throughout a representative day for each month of the year, then sums these to produce monthly and annual totals.

Step-by-step

  • Enter your location — latitude, longitude, and timezone. Negative latitude = southern hemisphere.
  • Set the day of month — this representative day is used for all 12 months (e.g. the 15th).
  • Configure your module — set tilt angle, surface area, and conversion efficiency.
  • Click Calculate — results appear instantly: monthly chart, breakdown table, and daily irradiance curve.
  • Download Excel — export all monthly and annual results to a spreadsheet.

Input Parameters

Latitude (°)
Location latitude. Negative = south of equator (e.g. −27.5 for Brisbane).
Longitude (°)
Location longitude. Positive = east of Greenwich (e.g. 153.0 for Brisbane).
Timezone (hrs)
Hours east of Greenwich Mean Time. E.g. Australia/Brisbane = +10.
Day of Month
Representative day used for each month's calculation. Default: 15.
Module Tilt (°)
Positive = tilted south; negative = tilted north.
Module Area (m²)
Total panel surface area in square metres.
Efficiency (%)
PV conversion efficiency. Typical crystalline silicon: 15–22%. Thin-film: 10–13%.

How the Calculation Works

For each 12-minute interval of the representative day, the model computes:

1. Solar position → Zenith angle (θz) and Azimuth via Spencer/EoT algorithm 2. Air mass → AM = √(1 + (sx²+sy²)/sz²) 3. Atmospheric attenuation → τ = AM^0.678 4. Direct beam irradiance → I_beam = 1.353 × 0.7^τ [kW/m²] 5. Diffuse irradiance → I_diff = 0.1 × I_beam × (1+cos θ_tilt)/(1+cos θz) 6. Module output → (I_beam·cos θ_inc + I_diff) × Area × Efficiency 7. Daily total = Σ(step outputs × 0.2 h)

The solar irradiance constant used is 1.353 kW/m² (extraterrestrial). The 0.7 factor represents average clear-sky atmospheric transmittance.

Important Notes

  • This is a clear-sky model — it does not account for clouds, dust, or shading. Real-world output will be lower.
  • The model assumes a fixed-tilt, south/north-facing panel with no tracking.
  • Annual totals use 31 days for Jan/Mar/May/Jul/Aug/Oct/Dec, 30 for Apr/Jun/Sep/Nov, and 28 for Feb.
